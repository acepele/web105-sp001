I graduated from Augustana College IL, with a BA in Economics. After college I worked in finance, with a focus in Foreign Exchange. I worked in Chicago, New York and most recently San Francisco.
Read on for some little known facts about these 3 cities.

 

Chicago rests on 234 square miles of land. The name Chicago was first recorded in 1688, appearing as the name Chigagou. This is an Algonquian word that means 'onion field'

 

Did you know that over 800 languages are spoken in New York City; that New York City was the first capital of the United States and that The Empire State Building is struck by lightning approximately 23 times a year?

 

Denim jeans were invented by Levi Strauss, in San Francisco, for the Gold Rush miners.
Alcatraz offered hot water to the prisoner; a strategy to stop them from trying to escape in the frigid San Francisco Bay waters.
